Our Story

Post-Harvest Losses

Kenya loses over 1.9 million tons of agricultural produce equivalent to 150 billion Kenya shillings ever year due to poor post-harvest management. Most Kenyan farmers depend on direct sun drying which is tedious, unhyienic and affects the quality of products due to loss of nutrient value, attack from pests, diseases and dirt. Uneven drying causes products to rot in storage.
Poor drying of crops affects their nutrient value hence mostly affecting the health of pregnant women and children below 5 years who mainly have stunted growth. Food waste also produces methane and CO2 gas that causes global warming.
